* Mathematical & Simulation Questions

** TODO [MATH-001] Fixed-point quantization impact on spin updates
- *Context*: The lab papers define continuous-time dynamics. When implementing this on the ESP32-C6 RISC-V core, floating-point math may introduce too much calculation latency.
- *Question*: Can we map the simulation variables to 16-bit or 32-bit fixed-point integers without destabilizing the energy minimization landscape?
- *Discussion*: See discussion notes in =docs/papers/Erementchouk-etal-Self-ALDIM.pdf= regarding stability boundaries.

* Hardware & Interconnect Questions

** TODO [HW-001] Optimal communication peripheral for spin-state broadcasting
- *Context*: We need to minimize propagation delay when nodes broadcast their local spin values to neighboring nodes.
- *Question*: Should we prioritize a wired multidrop bus topology like CAN bus (TWAI), or will custom high-speed SPI chaining provide lower latency for a small cluster of ESP32-C6 DevBoards?
- *Suggested Experiments*: Benchmark basic raw packet round-trip times for both peripherals under high congestion.

** TODO [HW-002] Clock synchronization accuracy across nodes
- *Context*: A synchronous update model requires all distributed MCUs to step through integration intervals at identical intervals.
- *Question*: Is the internal clock drift of separate ESP32-C6 boards low enough to maintain synchronization over long execution runs, or do we need a physical master clock wire triggering a GPIO interrupt on all slave boards?

* Architectural & Protocol Questions

** TODO [ARCH-001] Handling packet loss in dense graph topologies
- *Context*: If wireless (802.15.4) or wired connections drop a packet during an annealing cycle, the network topology becomes temporarily altered.
- *Question*: Does the dynamical Ising machine model handle occasional packet drop inherently as system noise, or will it cause the simulation to diverge or stall?
